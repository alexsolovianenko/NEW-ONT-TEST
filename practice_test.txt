Knowledge

1. What was the primary reason Canada became involved in World War I in 1914?
a) To expand its territories overseas
b) Because it was allied directly with Germany
c) Because it was part of the British Empire
d) To gain economic advantages

2. Which of the following best describes the Schlieffen Plan?
a) A German strategy to avoid a two-front war
b) A British naval blockade plan
c) A French defensive maneuver
d) A Canadian troop deployment strategy

3. In World War I, what does the term "No Man's Land" refer to?
a) Neutral territory used for peace talks
b) Land between opposing trenches
c) A deserted battlefield after a truce
d) Enemy-occupied territory

4. What was the main function of the War Measures Act in Canada during WWI?
a) To conscript women into the workforce
b) To suspend civil rights and increase government control
c) To establish a national education plan
d) To ban international trade

5. Which statement about the Battle of Vimy Ridge (1917) is true?
a) It was Canada's first major defeat
b) It marked the beginning of trench warfare
c) It was a key victory symbolizing Canadian unity and strength
d) It was led entirely by British commanders

Application

6. Explain how Canada demonstrated the idea of "total war" during World War I.

7. Describe how Victory Bonds helped Canada fund the war effort.

8. Identify one way civilians supported soldiers on the front lines during WWI.

9. Explain how technological advancements changed how battles were fought during the war.

10. Describe how the role of women in Canada changed during WWI.

Thinking

11. Why is the Battle of Vimy Ridge considered a turning point for Canada?

12. How did the Conscription Crisis of 1917 reveal divisions within Canada?

13. Why was propaganda a powerful tool for the Canadian government during WWI?

14. Explain how trench warfare affected soldiers' morale and strategy.

15. How did Canada’s contributions to the war influence its independence from Britain after 1918?

Communication

16. How would you explain the main causes of World War I to someone with no prior knowledge?

17. What would be an effective way to present statistics about Canadian casualties during WWI?

18. If you were to create a museum exhibit about life in the trenches, what three elements would you include and why?

19. Describe how you would summarize women's contributions to WWI in a classroom presentation.

20. Suggest how to best communicate Canada’s growing national identity after the war.

**Solutions**
###Knowledge

1. c) Because it was part of the British Empire — Canada automatically joined when Britain declared war.

2. a) A German strategy to avoid a two-front war — it aimed to defeat France quickly before turning to Russia.

3. b) Land between opposing trenches — a deadly area constantly exposed to enemy fire.

4. b) To suspend civil rights and increase government control — it allowed censorship and internment.

5. c) It was a key victory symbolizing Canadian unity and strength — fought and won under Canadian command.

###Application

6. Canada practiced total war by mobilizing all resources — industry, agriculture, and citizens — to support the war.

7. Victory Bonds allowed citizens to lend money to the government, raising essential funds for weapons and supplies.

8. Civilians supported troops through rationing, writing letters, and sending care packages to boost morale.

9. Advancements such as tanks, gas, and machine guns changed warfare from open-field battles to deadly trench combat.

10. Women worked in factories, served as nurses, and proved capable in roles once reserved for men, leading to social change.

###Thinking

11. The Battle of Vimy Ridge united Canadian forces under Canadian leadership and became a symbol of national pride.

12. The Conscription Crisis exposed deep divisions between English and French Canadians regarding compulsory military service.

13. Propaganda was effective because it appealed to emotion, patriotism, and guilt to increase enlistment and public support.

14. Trench warfare lowered morale due to poor conditions, constant danger, and little movement on the front.

15. Canada’s independent achievements in battle led to greater recognition and autonomy within the British Empire.

###Communication

16. The main causes of WWI were militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism — triggered by the assassination of Archduke Ferdinand.

17. Using clear graphs and charts comparing casualties by year or battle helps audiences understand the war’s human cost.

18. A trench exhibit could include a life-sized trench model, soldiers’ letters, and authentic equipment to convey realism.

19. Emphasize how women contributed in munitions factories, as nurses, and through volunteer organizations supporting soldiers.

20. Use historical presentations with images, quotes, and veteran accounts to show how WWI built Canada’s national identity.
